SAMPLE DIGITAL PRIVACY PROTECTION ACT

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE
This Act may be cited as the "Digital Privacy Protection Act of 2024."

SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS
For purposes of this Act:
(1) "Personal data" means any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual.
(2) "Data processor" means any entity that processes personal data on behalf of a data controller.
(3) "Consent" means freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous indication of the data subject's wishes.

SECTION 3. SCOPE AND APPLICATION
This Act applies to:
(a) Any entity that processes personal data of residents within the jurisdiction;
(b) Any entity that offers goods or services to residents within the jurisdiction;
(c) Any entity that monitors the behavior of residents within the jurisdiction.

SECTION 4. LAWFUL BASIS FOR PROCESSING
Personal data shall be processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently. Processing shall be based on:
(a) Consent of the data subject;
(b) Performance of a contract;
(c) Compliance with legal obligations;
(d) Protection of vital interests;
(e) Performance of public interest tasks;
(f) Legitimate interests pursued by the controller.

SECTION 5. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS
Data subjects shall have the following rights:
(a) Right to be informed about data processing;
(b) Right of access to personal data;
(c) Right to rectification of inaccurate data;
(d) Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten");
(e) Right to restrict processing;
(f) Right to data portability;
(g) Right to object to processing;
(h) Rights related to automated decision-making.

SECTION 6. DATA PROTECTION OBLIGATIONS
Data controllers must:
(a) Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures;
(b) Conduct data protection impact assessments for high-risk processing;
(c) Maintain records of processing activities;
(d) Report data breaches within 72 hours to the supervisory authority;
(e) Notify affected individuals of high-risk breaches without undue delay.

SECTION 7. PENALTIES
Violations of this Act may result in:
(a) Administrative fines up to 4% of annual global turnover or $20 million, whichever is higher;
(b) Criminal penalties for willful violations;
(c) Civil liability for damages to affected individuals.

SECTION 8. ENFORCEMENT
The Data Protection Authority shall have the power to:
(a) Investigate violations;
(b) Issue binding decisions;
(c) Impose administrative fines;
(d) Refer criminal matters to prosecutors.

SECTION 9. EFFECTIVE DATE
This Act shall take effect 18 months after the date of enactment.

SECTION 10. SEVERABILITY
If any provision of this Act is held invalid, the remainder shall remain in effect.
